# Consulting

Martenweave consulting helps delivery teams establish a practical model and evidence layer around the work they already have: mappings, extracts, validation reports, tickets, decisions, and SAP context. It is designed to make delivery knowledge reusable and reviewable without asking a team to replace its established delivery methods or systems.

## Where we help

- **SAP migration:** establish a model baseline, test mapping and dataset assumptions early, and make readiness exceptions reviewable.
- **MDM and MDG delivery:** keep business definitions, configuration intent, mapping rules, and local exceptions connected without claiming to replace the platform.
- **Data governance:** connect ownership and policy decisions to the field-level delivery artefacts where teams actually make changes.
- **AMS:** turn recurring model and interface questions into traceable investigations, decisions, and controlled proposals.

## How we work

We begin with a bounded model slice and a concrete decision or delivery risk. We then make the evidence and relationships inspectable, run deterministic checks, and help the team review a small number of high-value findings. The objective is a reusable controlled workflow, not a slide deck detached from delivery.

## A proposition for consulting practices

For SAP, data, and transformation consultancies, Martenweave can become a reusable delivery asset rather than another client-specific workbook. We can work with your practice on a bounded, client-safe starting point that:

- turns a representative migration, MDM, governance, or AMS scenario into a repeatable model-review workflow;
- keeps your team’s domain judgement visible in canonical objects, decisions, rules, and approval trails;
- produces reviewable evidence for client stakeholders without handing decision-making to automation; and
- creates a foundation your practice can extend through delivery accelerators, domain packs, and agreed integration patterns.

The practical first step is a joint discovery around one delivery scenario, the evidence already available, the people who must review it, and the handover outcome your team wants to retain. See [Partnerships](/docs/partnerships.html) for the collaboration model.
